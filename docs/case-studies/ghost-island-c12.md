# Case Study: Ghost Island C-12 Investigation

## Overview

The Ghost Island C-12 Investigation was the first real campaign case that led to AI TRPG World Engine.

It began as an AI-assisted investigation TRPG about a retired sea captain, Joseph Marsh, who brought an old inherited maritime chart to Miskatonic University. The chart depicted an island absent from modern navigation records.

Over time, the investigation expanded from the alleged island into historical maritime incidents, insurance records, navigation anomalies, and a North Atlantic region internally designated Sector C-12.

The campaign exposed a practical failure mode in long-running LLM-driven roleplay: important NPCs gradually drifted away from their original motives and identities as the conversation context grew.

## Campaign Premise

- Genre: Investigation, cosmic horror, academic research, sandbox TRPG.
- Setting: 1960s New England, United States.
- Primary location: Miskatonic University.
- Player character: John Peter.
- Initial catalyst: Joseph Marsh's inherited chart showing an island missing from modern maps.

## Investigation Summary

John Peter, a graduate student in the Department of Geography at Miskatonic University, began by consulting Professor West and university maritime archives.

The investigation expanded through:

- Historical maritime charts.
- University archives.
- Port authority records.
- Lloyd's insurance references.
- Route deviation analysis.
- Comparative accident records from 1795-1801 and 1950-1960.

Repeated incidents appeared to cluster around a specific North Atlantic region, which became known in the campaign notes as Sector C-12.

## Working Natural Explanation

The campaign did not confirm a supernatural explanation.

The working hypothesis combined:

- Submarine ridge.
- Seasonal storms.
- Localized currents.
- Heavy fog.
- Unmapped or poorly documented hazards.

This follows the engine rule that unusual phenomena should first be tested against natural explanations.

## Character Drift Incident

Joseph Marsh was originally defined as a retired sea captain whose central concern was his father's chart and the possible existence of the island.

During extended play, he gradually began behaving more like an academic researcher focused on statistical analysis. This contradicted his occupation, motive, and emotional center.

This was the first documented Character Drift case for the project.

Original motivation:

- Ghost Island.
- Father's chart.
- Closure regarding his father's obsession.

Drifted behavior:

- Academic research priorities.
- Statistical framing.
- Reduced focus on the island itself.

## Engine Lesson

The key conclusion was:

```text
Conversation Logs != World State
```

Long-running AI TRPG play needs durable, structured state files:

- World state.
- Timeline.
- Player state.
- NPC files.
- Clue database.
- Session log.

The Ghost Island C-12 Investigation became the first reference case for AI TRPG World Engine.

## Design Impact

This case directly shaped the engine's core principles:

- NPCs must have bounded knowledge.
- NPCs must retain stable goals and motivations.
- Hidden information must not leak through narration.
- Clues must distinguish facts, inference, speculation, and rumor.
- Timeline entries must be append-only.
- Session logs are not memory; they are records of observed events.

## Current Campaign Phase

Archived origin case:

- The campaign is no longer treated as a fully reliable playable reconstruction.
- Its primary value is documenting why AI TRPG World Engine was created.
- Future play should begin from a clean save created under current ATWE rules.

Phase 1 had completed:

- Historical investigation.
- Maritime archive research.
- C-12 statistical analysis.

Phase 2 was considered but not continued in this save:

- Ghost Island investigation.
- Correlation analysis.
- Expedition planning.

This case study is public project history. The playable save remains local by default and is ignored by Git.
